Experience Design: Totem Journey

Making indigenous culture accessible through immersive storytelling at UBC MOA

This project proposes an interactive experience for the Museum of Anthropology at UBC. Given an (intentionally) ill-defined brief, we conducted field research, framed and reframed the problem, generated user personas, and created a solution in the form of an interactive and discovery-oriented exhibit. Each step in the process was documented and presented with a professional slide deck.

[ Project was done in a team of four without any defined roles. The only divided up part was some of the final execution: for instance I did map illustrations while other people did other illustrations and user profiles.]

Summary of the design proposal

In zone 1, visitors walk through the dimly lit interior of a longhouse and experience video presentations. In zone 2, they can explore on their own in a simulated outdoor area and use totem pole miniatures to activate various audio-video presentations.
